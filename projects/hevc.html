Introduction of HEVC

HEVC is the most recent joint video project of the ITU-T Video Coding Experts Group (VCEG) and the ISO/IEC Moving Picture Experts Group (MPEG). HEVC has been designed to address essentially all existing applications of H.264/MPEG-4 AVCand to particularly focus on two key issues: increased video resolution and increased use of parallel processing architectures.It also significantly improved compression performance relative to existing standards.

Rate Control

Rate control plays an important role in video applications, especially in real time communication applications because the compressed video should be, in general, transmitted through a limited bandwidth. Therefore, it affects to prevent over- and under-flowing of buffer in the decoder. HEVC in the real market would be used with the rate control module for serving the high quality services. Practically, many kinds of rate control algorithms have been studied in standards such as TM5 for MPEG-2, VM8 for MPEG-4, and JVT-N046 for H.264/AVC.

Proposed Algorithm

Rate Control for Screen Content Coding in HEVC

Screen content usually has much different motion characteristics compared with conventional videos, which makesexisting rate control schemes unsuitable. The topic aims to find the optimal algorithms to ensure encoding distortionand better coding performance based on existing channel conditions, through researching the motion characteristics ofscreen content and the existing rate control algorithms.

Introduction of this Algorithm

As for bit allocation, I calculated the complexity by motion estimation and added sliding window to store the complexity. When implementing the bit allocation, I adjusted the threshold by complexity setting. By C++ coding, simulation on HM platform, constant debugging and optimizing, the algorithm was fully implemented. The new rate control algorithm is able to control bitrate accurately and improve the quality of the entire video sequence. And the experimental results show that the new algorithm achieves smaller bitrate errors and better coding performance.
